# Values Interview Prep — STAR Framework

> Separate from system design. Uses **STAR**: **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult

---

## Format
- Provide **detailed** examples — don't be vague
- Not evaluating technical skills — evaluating **alignment with company values**
- As a senior: show **ownership, initiative, and impact**

---

## Four Values to Prepare For

### 1. Ownership — "You'll Never Own a Ship Without Initiative"
**They'll ask:** A time you went beyond your duties, identified gaps, and drove improvements.

- [ ] Prepare an example of **proactive initiative** (not assigned — self-driven)
- [ ] Show **end-to-end ownership** (didn't hand off, saw it through)
- [ ] Demonstrate **impact** (measurable outcome)
- [ ] Senior signal: you didn't wait to be told, you identified and acted

### 2. Customer First — Prioritization & Customer Focus
**They'll ask:** Trade-offs, managing incidents, decisions under pressure where customer impact was key.

- [ ] Prepare an example of **balancing priorities with customer focus**
- [ ] Think about a **specific incident or system failure** and how you handled it
- [ ] Show **customer-centric decision making** under pressure
- [ ] Demonstrate that customer impact was always the **primary factor**

### 3. Athlete Mindset — Quality vs. Deadline, Continuous Improvement
**They'll ask:** A difficult technical or strategic trade-off under a tight deadline.

- [ ] Prepare an example of navigating **quality vs. speed** trade-offs
- [ ] Show how you maintained **high standards under pressure**
- [ ] Demonstrate **continuous improvement** mindset (not just shipping)

### 4. Growth Mindset — Feedback & Learning
**They'll ask:** Specific feedback you received. What did you learn?

- [ ] Prepare a **specific piece of feedback** and what you learned from it
- [ ] Show you **actively seek feedback** (don't wait for it)
- [ ] Explain how you **deliver feedback** to peers and junior engineers
- [ ] Demonstrate **growth trajectory** — how feedback changed your approach
